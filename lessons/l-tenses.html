Tenses

Tenses are grammatical tools we use in English to show when an action happens â€” in the past, present, or future.

Present Tenses

  • 1. Present Simple: Subject + base verb (s/es for he/she/it)
    Usage: habits, facts, schedules
    Example: She walks to school.
  • 2. Present Continuous: am/is/are + verb-ing
    Usage: actions happening now or planned
    Example: She is walking to school.
  • 3. Present Perfect: has/have + past participle
    Usage: actions with present results
    Example: She has walked to school.
  • 4. Present Perfect Continuous: has/have been + verb-ing
    Usage: actions continuing up to now
    Example: She has been walking to school.

Past Tenses

  • 5. Past Simple: Subject + past verb
    Usage: completed past actions
    Example: She walked to school.
  • 6. Past Continuous: was/were + verb-ing
    Usage: ongoing actions in the past
    Example: She was walking to school.
  • 7. Past Perfect: had + past participle
    Usage: action before another past action
    Example: She had walked to school before the bell rang.
  • 8. Past Perfect Continuous: had been + verb-ing
    Usage: duration of an action before something in the past
    Example: She had been walking for 30 minutes.

Future Tenses

  • 9. Future Simple: will + base verb
    Usage: predictions or promises
    Example: She will walk to school.
  • 10. Future Continuous: will be + verb-ing
    Usage: actions happening at a specific time in the future
    Example: She will be walking to school at 8 a.m.
  • 11. Future Perfect: will have + past participle
    Usage: action completed before a future point
    Example: She will have walked to school by 9 a.m.
  • 12. Future Perfect Continuous: will have been + verb-ing
    Usage: emphasizes duration up to a point in the future
    Example: She will have been walking for 30 minutes by 8:30.
